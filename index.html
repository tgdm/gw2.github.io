This is the simplest of the builds posted on this page. Your role is to cure conditions, heal, revive, and then offer a tiny bit of utility via damage, cc, and buffing allies. Before going a bit further, it is very important to understand that using any of the Overload skills will put that attunement on a long cooldown.

Your primary role in a fight is going to be to heal and cleanse, meaning you're going to be camping
(see skill breakdown below). You will be so reliant on this attunement that you should save your healing skill (
) for the rare times you swap out to other attunements. Your ability to apply and keep up Regeneration will determine how useful you are to your squad.

Let's break it down further per attunement in order of priority...

Water: You will primarily be camping this attunement in fights. All five of these skills are useful (six counting Overload).
  • (Water #1) While the damage output on this skill is negligible, by not having a target selected you can direct this "attack" at the ground to heal allies instead. The heal radius is relatively generous and affects 5 targets as well.
  • (Water #2) Decent damage, but more importantly (since this build has pretty much no damage) it applies Vulnerability to 5 targets as well as being a Combo Finisher: Blast. Excellent for blasting your own fields or those of allies.
  • (Water #3) Your burst heal and a tool to help revive players. Combining this with a manual revive will greatly speed up how quickly your allies are back on their feet. Also creates Combo Field: Water.
  • (Water #4) Area of effect Chill, unblockable. Leaves behind Combo Field: Ice which isn't particularly useful but still a neat way to apply Frost Aura to allies on the offensive.
  • (Water #5) Your large-scale method of applying Regeneration at range, creating a large Combo Field: Water, but capped at 5 targets. With a longer cooldown, use of this skill should be thought out as opposed to throwing it out right away.
  • (Overload) Decent pulsing heals which grant Regeneration and Vigor for up to 10 allies as well as Swiftness, Protection, and breaking stuns on yourself. The final pulse of this skill heals nearly 10 times as much. Be very mindful you will not have access to the Water healing skills for an extended time if you use this Overload and swap attunements.

Earth: A defensive attunement with utility options.
  • (Earth #1) Not particularly worth using, but I will note that it applies Weakness and is a Combo Finisher: Physical Projectile.
  • (Earth #2) Applies Bleeding, Cripple, and a decent bit of damage all wrapped in Combo Finisher: Blast. This ability has a decent radius with a 5 target limit, but also has a longer cast time and takes 3 seconds to actually detonate. Use offensively to blast fields and cripple enemies.

Air: A utility attunement with an offensive emphasis.

  • (Air #3) Knockback up to 5 targets. Very finnicky to use and can have the adverse effect of pushing enemies out of squad damage. Use sparingly and defensively.
  • (Air #4) This is where Air starts to get good! Applies Swiftness to allies and removes Crippled, Immobilized, and Chilled.
  • (Air #5) An unblockable f ield which stuns enemies crossing it (up to 10 targets). Incredibly useful CC and the Combo Field: Lightning on the side. Try to cycle this into a fight whenever you can, but not at the cost of healing allies.
  • (Overload) This one is heavily focused on offensive. It applies Vulnerability (up to 3 targets) per pulse which is pretty underwhelming, but for Power-based damage the Static Charge effect applied on the final pulse is like having a free Superior Sigil of Air proc. The Combo Field: Lightning can be blasted for Swiftness.

Fire: A purely offensive attunement.
  • (Fire #1) Good damage with a cleave in a small radius. Filler damage if you get the chance for it.
  • (Fire #2) Primary damage source and Combo Field: Fire for blasting Might to allies.
  • (Fire #3) Applies Burning to 5 targets. It's meh, but you can do some fancy animation cancelling with it and Lava Font.
  • (Fire #4) Roll backward and leave behind a Combo Field: Fire. Although the Evade granted by it is only 1 second long, the mobility it grants makes it a powerful defensive tool.
  • (Fire #5) One of the core damaging skills for our Weaver zerg build, nearly pointless to use with this support Tempest build. The channel leaves you very vulnerable and you will not spike significant damage with the build you're using. Avoid using this skill.
  • (Overload) Pulse Might to allies and leave behind a Combo Field: Fire. Useful to use before engaging to apply Might to allies, nearly pointless to use in the course of an actual fight.
